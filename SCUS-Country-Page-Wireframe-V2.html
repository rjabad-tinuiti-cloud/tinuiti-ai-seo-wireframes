Crisis in Gaza: What to Know
What does it mean that famine has been confirmed in Gaza?
Famine is the most extreme level of hunger, defined by the Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (IPC). It is confirmed when all three conditions are met: At least 20% of households face extreme shortages, 15%+ of children are acutely malnourished, and at least two people per 10,000 die each day from hunger.
Has the UN concluded that genocide is occurring in Gaza?
Yes. A UN commission concluded, for the first time, that genocide is being committed in Gaza. The inhumanity of the situation is unconscionable. As humanitarian leaders, we have borne direct witness to the horrifying deaths and suffering.
What is the current humanitarian situation in Gaza?
The situation in Gaza is catastrophic. Children are literally starving. Markets are empty, food is unavailable, and malnutrition is widespread. Save the Children teams are seeing a sharp rise in malnourished children at our clinics.
Are you currently on the ground in Gaza?
Yes, our teams are actively distributing aid, providing clean drinking water in 26 communities daily, and operating two primary healthcare centers.
